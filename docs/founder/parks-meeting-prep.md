# Parks Meeting Prep

## Main Goal
Leave the meeting with clarity on:
- whether Parks could become a real execution-side partner
- what kinds of files they actually want
- whether Peregrine could live in or around their structure
- what a good handoff from FALCO to operator looks like

## Simple Explanation
Use this first:

FALCO finds distressed-property files early, cleans them up, and turns the strongest ones into private review briefs for approved partners. The point is not to claim every file is ready to move. The point is to get the right files in front of serious operators earlier and in a cleaner format than raw notice scraping.

If Peregrine comes up:

Peregrine is the licensed execution home. FALCO is the origination and screening system. Peregrine is where the right files can actually be worked through the proper channel.

## What To Show
Bring only a few examples:
- one file that held up in real-world validation
- one file that was real but too late or too controlled
- one strong upstream or pre-foreclosure example

Do not overload the meeting with too many packets.

## What You Need To Learn
Ask these directly:
- What kinds of files are actually worth your time?
- What makes a file too late or too controlled?
- What would make a file feel early enough to work?
- What would a useful handoff from FALCO look like on your side?
- Where do you think you could fit:
  - feedback
  - review
  - execution
  - brokerage home
- If Peregrine were going to live somewhere sensible, what would that look like?
- What would make that a bad fit?

## What To Listen For
You are trying to hear:
- what they want more of
- what they will not touch
- how they talk about control, timing, and owner agency
- whether they are thinking like a user, partner, or possible host

## Good Outcome
A good meeting is not just "they like it."

A good meeting means:
- they understand FALCO quickly
- they define what they want more clearly
- they explain what an ideal file looks like
- they tell you whether Peregrine has a plausible place with them
- they leave open the next concrete step

## Bad Outcome
Watch for:
- vague encouragement with no real fit
- interest in the concept but no workflow interest
- confusion between FALCO and Peregrine
- broad praise without operational specifics

## Your Tone
Be direct, calm, and coachable.
Do not oversell.
Do not claim the machine is finished.
Frame this as:
- serious system
- still being sharpened
- built around operator truth

## Close The Meeting With
Use something like:

I am trying to get this right around the way real operators actually work, not just around what sounds good. If there is a sensible role here for Parks, FALCO, and possibly Peregrine, I want to understand it clearly.

## Immediate Follow-Up
Right after the meeting, write down:
- what they liked
- what they did not like
- what file profile they want
- what role they may want
- whether Peregrine fit sounded real or not
