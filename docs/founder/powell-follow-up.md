# Powell Follow-Up

## Main Goal
Find out whether Powell sees FALCO as:
- actually useful
- repeatedly useful
- worth staying close to

You are trying to separate real workflow value from simple curiosity.

## Opening
Use something simple:

I wanted to follow up after you had some time with the system and see what actually felt useful, what did not, and what would make it stronger for your team.

## Questions To Ask
- Which files or packets felt strongest?
- What felt useful right away?
- What felt confusing or unnecessary?
- Did the vault and packet flow make sense?
- What information did you wish was clearer sooner?
- Which files felt real but not workable?
- What would make you come back and use this regularly?
- What would make this genuinely useful for Powell instead of just interesting?

## What You Need To Learn
You are looking for:
- whether the product is legible
- whether the output quality is high enough
- whether they see repeat use
- what specific changes increase usefulness
- whether they are thinking as a possible real partner

## How To Frame The Product
Keep it simple:

FALCO is the upstream screening and review layer. The goal is to find and clean up the right distress files early, then put the strongest ones in front of the right operators for validation.

## What Not To Do
- do not pitch too hard
- do not ask for commitment too early
- do not defend every part of the product
- do not treat polite feedback as strong validation

## Best Signals
Strong signals:
- they want more files
- they want stronger fit, not just a cleaner website
- they tell you exactly how they would use it
- they compare it against their real workflow
- they want to influence how it develops

Weak signals:
- generic praise
- "interesting" with no concrete use case
- no clear file preferences
- no follow-through

## Good Outcome
By the end of the conversation, you should know:
- what Powell found valuable
- what they did not
- whether they see repeat workflow value
- what would make it materially better for them

## Immediate Follow-Up
After the conversation, write down:
- strongest positive signal
- strongest point of confusion
- what type of files they want
- what one improvement would help most
- whether they feel like a real partner candidate
